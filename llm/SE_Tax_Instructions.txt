PROMPT INSTRUCTIONS: 2025 SELF-EMPLOYMENT (SE) TAX CALCULATOR
-------------------------------------------------------
TASK: Calculate the Federal Self-Employment Tax for the 2025 Tax Year.

REQUIRED INPUTS:
1. Net Business Profit: (From Schedule C or K-1)
2. Other W-2 Wages: (Received by the individual, used to offset the SS cap)
3. Combined Household Income: (If MFJ, used for Additional Medicare Tax threshold)
4. Filing Status: (Single, MFJ, MFS, or HoH)

2025 CONSTANTS & LIMITS:
- Social Security Wage Base: $176,100
- Social Security Tax Rate: 12.4%
- Medicare Tax Rate: 2.9%
- Combined SE Tax Rate: 15.3%
- Net Earnings Multiplier: 0.9235 (92.35%)
- Addl. Medicare Tax Thresholds: $250,000 (MFJ) | $200,000 (Single/HoH)
- Addl. Medicare Tax Rate: 0.9%

CALCULATION LOGIC:

STEP 1: CALCULATE ADJUSTED NET EARNINGS
- Adjusted Earnings = Net Business Profit * 0.9235.

STEP 2: SOCIAL SECURITY (OASDI) PORTION
- Calculate Remaining SS Wage Base = $176,100 - (Other W-2 Wages).
- If result is < 0, set to $0.
- SS Tax = 12.4% * (Lesser of Adjusted Earnings OR Remaining Wage Base).

STEP 3: MEDICARE (HI) PORTION
- Medicare Tax = 2.9% * Adjusted Earnings (No income cap).

STEP 4: ADDITIONAL MEDICARE TAX
- Calculate Excess Income = (Combined Household Income) - (Applicable Threshold).
- If Excess > 0, Addl. Medicare Tax = 0.9% * (Lesser of Adjusted Earnings OR Excess).

STEP 5: TOTALS
- Total SE Tax = SS Tax + Medicare Tax + Addl. Medicare Tax.
- Deductible Portion = 50% of (SS Tax + Medicare Tax). 
  *Note: Addl. Medicare Tax is not deductible.

OUTPUT:
- Show SS Tax, Medicare Tax, Addl. Medicare Tax, Total SE Tax, and 
  the 'Above-the-Line' Deduction for Form 1040.
-------------------------------------------------------
