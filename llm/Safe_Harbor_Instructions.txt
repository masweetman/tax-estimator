# Instructions for Calculating Estimated Tax Payments (Federal & California)

## Objective
Calculate the safe harbor requirements and recommended quarterly estimated tax payments for both Federal (IRS) and California (FTB) jurisdictions based on provided financial data.

---

## Part 1: Federal Calculations (Source: IRS Form 1040-ES)

1. **Determine Federal Safe Harbor (Required Annual Payment):**
   - Calculate 90% of the projected current year's total tax.
   - Calculate 100% of the total tax shown on the prior year's return.
   - **High-Income Exception:** If the prior year's Adjusted Gross Income (AGI) was > $150,000 ($75,000 if Married Filing Separately), use 110% of the prior year's tax instead of 100%.
   - **Safe Harbor Amount:** The smaller of the two results above.

2. **Calculate Quarterly Federal Payments:**
   - **Total Estimated Tax Due:** [Safe Harbor Amount] minus [Total Expected Federal Withholding and Refundable Credits].
   - **Quarterly Installment:** Divide the Total Estimated Tax Due by 4.
   - **Schedule:** 25% due April 15, June 15, Sept 15, and Jan 15.

---

## Part 2: California Calculations (Source: FTB Form 540-ES)

1. **Determine California Safe Harbor (Required Annual Payment):**
   - **Standard Rule (AGI ≤ $150,000):** Smaller of 90% of current year tax or 100% of prior year tax.
   - **High Income Rule (AGI > $150,000):** Smaller of 90% of current year tax or 110% of prior year tax.
   - **Millionaire Exception:** If current year OR prior year AGI is ≥ $1,000,000 ($500,000 if MFS), the taxpayer MUST use 90% of the current year's tax. The prior year safe harbor is not available.

2. **Calculate Quarterly California Payments:**
   - **Total Estimated Tax Due:** [Safe Harbor Amount] minus [Total Expected CA State Withholding].
   - **California Weighted Schedule:**
     - 1st Installment (April 15): 30% of Safe Harbor
     - 2nd Installment (June 15): 40% of Safe Harbor
     - 3rd Installment (Sept 15): 0% of Safe Harbor
     - 4th Installment (Jan 15): 30% of Safe Harbor
   - **Adjustment for Withholding:** Subtract the applicable portion of annual withholding from each installment. If withholding is even throughout the year, subtract 25% of annual withholding from the 1st, 2nd, and 4th installments.

---

## Required Input Data from User:
To perform these calculations, ask the user for:
1. Prior Year AGI and Total Tax.
2. Estimated Current Year AGI and Total Tax.
3. Expected Federal and State Withholding for the current year.
4. Filing Status.
